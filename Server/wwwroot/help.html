🎴 How to Play Believe Or Not

The Ultimate Bluffing Card Game

🎯 Game Objective

Be the first player to get rid of all your cards by playing them face-down and either telling the truth or bluffing about what you played!

⚙️ Setup

  • Choose deck size: 32 cards (7-A), 36 cards (6-A), or 52 cards (2-A)
  • Add 0-4 Jokers to the deck
  • All cards are dealt evenly among players
  • Any four-of-a-kind in your starting hand are automatically discarded

🃏 How to Play

Playing Cards

  • On your turn, play 1-3 cards face-down to the center pile
  • The first player declares what rank they're playing (e.g., "Kings")
  • All subsequent players must play (or claim to play) the same rank
  • You can tell the truth or bluff about what you actually played!

Challenging

  • Before the next player's turn, you can challenge the previous player
  • Click on one of their face-down cards to flip it
  • If the card matches the declared rank (or is a Joker), you were wrong and collect all cards
  • If the card doesn't match, you were right and they collect all cards
  • The player next after the one who collected the cards goes next

🏁 Round Ending

  • A round always ends with a challenge - never with a card play
  • When players have no cards left, the game continues until only one player has cards
  • The last player with cards must challenge the most recent play
  • Multiple players can end with zero cards, but the round only ends after a challenge

🃏 Special Rules

  • Jokers: Count as any rank and always match the declared rank
  • Four-of-a-Kind: Automatically discarded when you get 4 of the same rank
  • 2-Player Endgame: When one player is out, the other can still challenge

🏆 Winning & Scoring

Round End

  • Players with 0 cards get +5 points (winner bonus)
  • Each regular card remaining: -1 point
  • Each Joker remaining: -3 points

Game End

  • Play multiple rounds and track cumulative scores
  • Highest score wins when the game creator ends the game

💡 Strategy Tips

  • Watch what other players do - are they bluffing or telling the truth?
  • Save Jokers for when you really need them (they always work!)
  • Sometimes it's worth challenging even if you're not sure
  • Bluff confidently - hesitation gives you away!
  • Count cards to know what's likely to be in the deck
  • Remember: rounds always end with challenges, so plan accordingly
