üëë The King - Game Rules

Traditional Russian Card Game

üéØ Game Overview

The King is a traditional Russian trick-taking card game played with 4 players using a standard 52-card deck. Each player plays for themselves, trying to score the least points possible across 8 different rounds.

üÉè Basic Setup

  • Players: Exactly 4 players
  • Cards: Standard 52-card deck (no jokers)
  • Deal: 13 cards per player each round
  • Objective: Score the fewest points across all 8 rounds

üîÑ The 8 Rounds

Each game consists of exactly 8 rounds, each with different scoring rules:

  1. Round 1: No Hearts - Avoid taking hearts (each heart = 1 point)
  2. Round 2: No Queens - Avoid taking queens (each queen = 5 points)
  3. Round 3: No Boys - Avoid taking jacks and kings (each = 2 points)
  4. Round 4: No King of Spades - Avoid the King of Spades (= 10 points)
  5. Round 5: No Last Two Tricks - Avoid winning the last 2 tricks (each = 10 points)
  6. Round 6: No Tricks - Avoid taking any tricks (each trick = 2 points)
  7. Round 7: Everything Bad - All previous penalties combined!
  8. Round 8: Choose Your Poison - King chooses which round type to repeat

‚ô† Trump Selection

At the start of each round, one player becomes the "King" and chooses the trump suit:

  • The King rotates each round (player 1, then 2, then 3, then 4, repeat)
  • Trump cards beat all non-trump cards
  • Higher trump cards beat lower trump cards
  • Choose wisely - trump selection affects the entire round!

üéÆ How to Play

Playing Tricks:

  • The King leads the first trick
  • Players must follow suit if possible
  • If you can't follow suit, you may play any card (including trump)
  • Highest card of the led suit wins (unless trumped)
  • Winner of each trick leads the next trick

Card Ranking:

High to Low: Ace, King, Queen, Jack, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2

üèÜ Scoring & Winning

  • Lower scores are better - you want the fewest points!
  • Points are awarded based on the round type
  • After 8 rounds, the player with the lowest total score wins
  • If tied, the player who was King fewer times wins

üí° Strategy Tips

  • Count cards: Remember what's been played
  • Trump timing: Save high trumps for crucial moments
  • Force others: Lead suits others don't have
  • Plan ahead: Sometimes taking points early prevents bigger penalties later
  • Watch the King: In Round 8, anticipate what the King might choose

‚ö† Important Rules

  • You must follow suit if you have cards of the led suit
  • Hearts cannot be led until hearts have been "broken" (played on a trick)
  • The Queen of Spades breaks hearts automatically
  • In Round 7, all penalties apply - be extra careful!
  • Only the game creator can end a round early
